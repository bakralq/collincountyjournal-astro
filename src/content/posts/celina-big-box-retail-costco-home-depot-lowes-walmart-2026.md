For years, Celina residents asked the same question in different ways.

When will we stop driving somewhere else for basic shopping?

That question is changing.

Celina's first Walmart Supercenter opens April 29. Lowe's is already operating on South Preston Road. Home Depot is under construction. Costco is being tracked through heavy site work.

The dominant pattern is no longer rumor.

It is anchor retail.

## Quick Read

- Walmart opens April 29 at 1771 N. Preston Road.
- Lowe's lists an operating Celina store at 3540 S. Preston Road.
- Celina Watch reported in February that Walmart was in final interior finish-out with an April 2026 target opening.
- Celina Watch also reported Home Depot was in rapid vertical construction with an August 2026 target opening.
- The same tracker listed Costco in heavy civil and utility work with a late Q4 2026 target.
- Texas Real Estate Research Center reported Shawnee Trail is planned as a 100-acre, more than $1 billion mixed-use development anchored by a 200,000-square-foot Walmart.
- The issue now is not whether big-box retail is coming. It is how much traffic and secondary development will follow.

## The Big Shift

Celina is moving from a city that talks about major retail to a city that has to manage major retail.

That is a different stage of growth.

Before the stores arrive, the conversation is convenience.

After they arrive, the conversation becomes traffic, access, nearby restaurants, drainage, police calls, parking lots, delivery trucks, sales tax, and small business pressure.

The benefit is obvious.

Residents get more local options.

The tradeoff is also obvious.

The shopping patterns of surrounding communities may now point toward Celina.

## What We Know

The [Celina Chamber](https://business.celinachamber.org/event-calendar/Details/walmart-supercenter-celina-grand-opening-ribbon-cutting-1670461?rendermode=print) lists Walmart's grand opening and ribbon cutting for April 29.

[Lowe's](https://www.lowes.com/store/3528-celina-tx/tool-store) lists its Celina location at 3540 S. Preston Road.

[Celina Watch](https://celinawatch.com/the-blueprint/issue-002/) reported that Walmart was in final interior finish-out earlier this year, while Home Depot was in rapid vertical construction and Costco was in heavy civil and utility work.

The [Texas Real Estate Research Center](https://trerc.tamu.edu/news-talk/billion-dollar-mixed-use-development-underway-in-celina/) reported that Shawnee Trail is expected to span 100 acres and include retail, entertainment, housing, green space, office space, restaurants, an amphitheater, and 600 apartments.

That makes Walmart the first major visible anchor in a larger commercial pattern.

## Why Residents Care

The public usually evaluates these projects through daily life.

Will I save time?

Will I get better prices?

Will I have to sit longer at Preston?

Will local businesses lose customers or gain traffic?

Will we finally get more restaurants because anchors are here?

Those are the right questions because large retailers do not operate alone. They pull smaller development behind them.

## The Small Business Pressure

Big-box retail can help local businesses by bringing more people into Celina.

It can also hurt businesses that compete directly on price, convenience, or product category.

The city should expect both effects.

That is why sales tax growth does not tell the full story. Residents should watch which local shops benefit from more traffic and which ones face more pressure.

## The Next Gap

The big-box question is no longer whether Celina can attract major names.

It can.

The next question is what daily-life gaps remain after those anchors arrive. Residents may still leave town for restaurants, specialty grocery, medical appointments, entertainment, clothing, furniture, fitness, or late-night options.

That is where the next wave of local demand will show up.

## Bottom Line

Celina's major retail era has moved from "coming soon" to opening dates, construction, and visible site work.

The convenience will be real.

So will the pressure on roads, small businesses, and daily traffic patterns. The next year will show whether Celina can absorb big-box retail as smoothly as it attracted it.
