The question is simple because residents see it from the road.

What are they building near the Melissa H-E-B?

A public Reddit post asked that exact question on April 24. The better answer is not one single building. It is that the H-E-B area sits inside a larger Sam Rayburn Highway and U.S. 75 commercial buildout.

The dominant pattern is corridor growth.

H-E-B did not arrive alone.

## Quick Read

- Residents are publicly asking what is being built near the Melissa H-E-B.
- H-E-B opened its Melissa store in May 2025 at 1230 Central Expressway, according to Community Impact.
- Melissa Park Village site items recently moved through Planning and Zoning for land near Sam Rayburn Highway and Liberty Way.
- A LoopNet listing shows 10,000 square feet of office/retail space at 3316 Sam Rayburn Highway with availability listed for June 1, 2026.
- Melissa's 121 corridor is filling with retail, restaurant, office, medical, and service uses.
- The safest answer: some specific tenants may still be unconfirmed, but the public records show a larger commercial district forming.

## The H-E-B Anchor

[Community Impact](https://communityimpact.com/dallas-fort-worth/mckinney/business/2025/05/14/h-e-b-sells-groceries-tru-texas-bbq-at-new-melissa-store/) reported that H-E-B Melissa opened May 14, 2025 in a 136,000-square-foot store at 1230 Central Expressway. The store includes grocery departments, a pharmacy, a True Texas BBQ restaurant, curbside, and delivery services.

That store changed the gravity of the area.

Major grocery anchors draw daily traffic.

Daily traffic draws pads, restaurants, medical offices, service tenants, and other retail.

That is why residents now see construction and ask what is coming next.

## Melissa Park Village

The clearest public planning signal is Melissa Park Village.

The April 9 [Planning and Zoning agenda](https://www.cityofmelissa.com/AgendaCenter/ViewFile/Agenda/_04092026-493) included multiple Melissa Park Village Final Engineering Site items. The sites are generally located at the northeast corner of Sam Rayburn Highway and Liberty Way, according to the city's agenda.

The listed lots included 2.72 acres, 2.67 acres, and 1.81 acres in the Melissa Park Village Addition.

Those are not vague rumors.

They are active site-level planning items.

## Other Corridor Activity

There is also private commercial listing activity.

A [LoopNet listing](https://www.loopnet.com/Listing/3316-Sam-Rayburn-Hwy-Melissa-TX/39678574/) for 3316 Sam Rayburn Highway describes 10,000 square feet of office/retail space available in a 2026 under-construction building, with June 1, 2026 listed as availability. The listing highlights exposure at 121 and Milrany, a signalized intersection, national or credit tenants nearby, and surrounding residential growth.

That kind of listing shows how developers are marketing the corridor.

They are not selling it as empty land.

They are selling it as a growth corridor with traffic, rooftops, schools, and major retailers nearby.

## Why Residents Care

The resident question is not only curiosity.

It affects traffic, jobs, errands, property values, school routes, and whether people still need to drive into McKinney for basic services.

Each new pad may seem small.

Together, the pads become a commercial district.

That is the pattern around H-E-B.

## What Residents Should Watch

The next useful signal will be tenant names.

Site work and commercial listings show where development is moving, but tenants determine how residents experience it. A medical office, restaurant, bank, daycare, drive-thru, or service business can all create different traffic patterns.

Residents should watch the intersections near 121, Milrany, Liberty Way, and H-E-B as each pad moves from planning to opening.

## Bottom Line

The public records do not support one clean answer for every building residents are seeing near Melissa H-E-B.

They do show a clear direction.

The area around H-E-B is becoming a larger retail and service corridor, with Melissa Park Village and other Sam Rayburn Highway projects moving through planning, leasing, and construction. Residents should expect more announcements, more convenience, and more traffic pressure in the same place.
