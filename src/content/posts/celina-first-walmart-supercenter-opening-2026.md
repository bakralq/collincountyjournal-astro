For years, Celina residents have left town for basic errands.

That changes this week in a visible way.

Celina's first Walmart Supercenter is scheduled to open Wednesday, April 29, with a grand opening and ribbon cutting from 7:30 a.m. to 9 a.m. at 1771 N. Preston Road, according to the [Celina Chamber of Commerce event listing](https://business.celinachamber.org/event-calendar/Details/walmart-supercenter-celina-grand-opening-ribbon-cutting-1670461?rendermode=print).

The opening is not only a retail event.

It is a growth marker.

The dominant number is 200,000 square feet. That is the size Texas Real Estate Research Center reported for the Walmart anchoring the first phase of Shawnee Trail, a 100-acre mixed-use development planned near Preston Road and the Collin County Outer Loop.

## Quick Read

- Celina's first Walmart Supercenter opens April 29 at 1771 N. Preston Road.
- The ribbon cutting is scheduled from 7:30 a.m. to 9 a.m.
- The Walmart anchors the first phase of Shawnee Trail, a larger mixed-use development.
- Texas Real Estate Research Center reported Shawnee Trail is planned as a 100-acre project with retail, entertainment, housing, green space, office space, restaurant and entertainment uses, an amphitheater, and 600 apartments.
- The practical questions now are traffic, access, nearby development, and whether residents still leave town for key services.

## The Pattern

Walmart is the first thing residents will feel.

The larger pattern is corridor conversion.

Preston Road and the Outer Loop area are becoming more than pass-through growth territory. They are becoming a retail and service destination for the city.

That is why this opening matters more than one store.

Once a Walmart Supercenter opens, surrounding land becomes more attractive for restaurants, service businesses, medical tenants, apartments, gas stations, and smaller retail pads. Residents notice that shift in two ways: more convenience and more traffic.

Both are real.

## What Comes With The Store

The [Texas Real Estate Research Center](https://trerc.tamu.edu/news-talk/billion-dollar-mixed-use-development-underway-in-celina/) reported that Shawnee Trail is expected to span 100 acres and combine retail, entertainment, housing, and green space. The same report said the first phase would include office space, a restaurant and entertainment complex, green space with an amphitheater, and 600 apartments.

That means the Walmart opening is the first public-facing piece of a larger buildout.

Residents should watch the site in stages.

First, daily shopping traffic.

Then, nearby pad activity.

Then, restaurant and entertainment announcements.

Then, apartment and mixed-use impacts.

That sequence is how major retail centers usually become daily traffic patterns.

## What Residents Will Care About

The questions will not be technical.

Residents will ask whether it saves time. Whether Preston gets worse. Whether grocery competition improves. Whether smaller businesses lose customers or gain traffic. Whether nearby roads can absorb opening-week activity.

Those are the right questions.

Celina has been asking for more retail for years. Now the retail is arriving in large-format form.

That creates convenience.

It also creates a new center of gravity.

## What Changes Next

The next test is daily use.

If the store pulls routine shopping trips back into Celina, residents may feel the benefit quickly. If traffic around Preston Road becomes heavier than expected, the convenience will come with a new cost.

The practical measure is simple: whether this opening saves residents more time than it takes from them.

## Bottom Line

Celina's first Walmart is a practical win for residents who have wanted closer shopping options.

It is also a signal that the city is entering a more intense retail phase.

The store opens April 29. The real test begins after the ribbon cutting, when the daily traffic, spending patterns, and next wave of development start showing residents what this part of Celina is becoming.
